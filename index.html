Mission & Purpose

To reduce preventable post-discharge regression by requiring demonstrated ("show-me") readiness before final sign-off.

"A transplant patient's life isn't saved in the operating room. It's saved at the discharge desk."

— S. Grimes, Transplant Recipient

Gap 01

Verified Readiness

Moving from knowledge to physical mastery before the patient leaves.

Gap 02

Standardized Validation

Mandatory "show-me" method — no exceptions, no shortcuts.

Gap 03

72-Hour Planning

Mapping the first 72 hours at home before the patient departs.

Gap 04

Ownership

Verified follow-ups confirmed before any exit is signed.

The Problem We're Solving

Most transplant failures
happen after discharge.

The surgery succeeds. The care team celebrates. The patient goes home. And then — without verified readiness, a support plan, or a follow-up structure in place — preventable crises unfold in the first 72 hours.

The Transplant Discharge Wellness Group exists because exceptional patient care does not end at the bedside. It extends through the front door, into the home, and through the first critical weeks of recovery. This training is how we make that real.
