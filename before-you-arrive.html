Before You Arrive

Please Read Before You Arrive

This is not a quiz. There are no trick questions. What you are about to read is a real scenario — the kind that happens when systems fail and no one speaks up. We are asking you to sit with it, and then respond to it honestly. Your response is anonymous. No one will judge what you write. But we ask that you write as if the person in this story is someone you love.

Serge's Story

A transplant patient. Alone. In a cab. Going home to an empty home with a bag of discharge paperwork he had never been walked through. He had survived hospice, life support, the oncology floor, and a 16-hour transplant surgery. Fifteen months on a waiting list. A second chance at life.

The operation went well. The care team was proud of the outcome. He thought he had already done the most difficult thing he'd ever have to do in his life — and he was so full of hope for the miracle that gave him a second chance. For him. And for his two sons.

But on the day of his discharge, no one came. Same with the day after. And the day after that.

Why didn't his family come? Why didn't anyone find out where they were?

Why didn't he have all the medications he needed for the next few days?

Why didn't he have a primary care physician?

Most of all — why was he now in a cab, and not still at the hospital?

Now It's Your Turn

Put someone you love into this story.

Your Reflection

Think of someone you love — a child, or if you don't have children, a parent. Put their name and their face into this story. They are the patient in the cab. Then answer these questions in your own words, honestly and anonymously.

What sequence of events, decisions, or gaps allowed Serge to leave that hospital alone and unprepared?
Think about systems, culture, communication, assumptions. What had to already be broken for this to happen?
This is the heart of why this training exists. Speak from that place.

Your response is completely anonymous. No name, no identifier, no judgment. This is your first act of membership in the Transplant Discharge Wellness Group.

✦

Thank you for your honesty.

Your response has been received anonymously. It will inform the work we do together in training — and the care every transplant patient receives from this day forward.

We'll see you there.
